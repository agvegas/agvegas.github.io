01 Services

Airworthiness Reviews issued under EASA Regulation (EU) 1321/2014, Annex Vb (Part ML). Covering the full Part ML scope — ELA1 and ELA2 aircraft, as well as other non-complex motor and piston aeroplanes within the regulation's limits — privately operated, club-owned, or under non-commercial operations.

Airworthiness Review (ARC)

Independent physical and documentary review of the aircraft in accordance with ML.A.901 and related AMC/GM.

  • Full documentation review: logs, ADs, SBs, modifications, repairs, weight & balance, mass & balance, limitations.
  • Physical inspection of the airframe, engine, propeller, avionics and emergency equipment.
  • Issue of the Airworthiness Review Certificate (ARC) or reasoned refusal.
  • Annual validity, aligned to the operator's schedule.

ARC Revisions & Extensions

Re-issue, correction, and extension of existing ARCs following Part ML provisions.

  • Re-review after major maintenance, modifications, or component changes.
  • Continuity of airworthiness in case of changes of owner or operator.
  • Liaison with the competent authority where required.

Continuing Airworthiness Support

Practical support for owners of ELA1 and ELA2 aircraft, and other non-complex aircraft within Part ML scope, running their continuing airworthiness under Part ML Subpart H (owner/operator management).

  • Maintenance programme development and tailoring.
  • ADs, SBs, SIs, SLs, SILs monitoring and control.
  • Pre-purchase and post-incident airworthiness review support.

03 ARC Process

A structured, transparent workflow so the owner always knows where the review stands and what is required next.

  1. 1. Scoping

    Aircraft type, registration, current status, last ARC, open items. NDA and quote.

  2. 2. Documentation

    Pre-review of all required records: maintenance programme, logs, ADs, SBs, modifications, repair records, mass & balance, flight manual supplements, pilot/operator declarations.

  3. 3. Physical review

    Inspection of the aircraft, including airframe, powerplant, propeller, avionics, emergency and survival equipment, markings, and required placards.

  4. 4. Findings

    Detailed report of any discrepancies classified as safety-relevant, non-safety-relevant, or documentation findings.

  5. 5. ARC issue

    Issue of the Airworthiness Review Certificate once all findings are closed, or reasoned refusal in line with Part ML.
